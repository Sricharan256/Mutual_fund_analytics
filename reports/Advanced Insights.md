# Advanced Insights

1. Funds with the highest VaR and CVaR exhibit significantly larger downside risk and may be unsuitable for conservative investors.

2. Rolling 90-day Sharpe ratios show that risk-adjusted performance changes over time and is not constant across market cycles.

3. Investor cohorts that entered during recent years contributed the highest investment amounts, indicating growing retail participation in mutual funds.

4. SIP continuity analysis identified investors with transaction gaps greater than 35 days, highlighting a segment at risk of discontinuing investments.

5. Equity funds with high HHI values are more concentrated and may carry higher portfolio concentration risk compared to diversified funds.
